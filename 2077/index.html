Cyberpunk 2077 is an open-world role-playing game developed by CD Projekt Red and set in a futuristic city called Night City. Players take on the role of ‘V,’ a mercenary, and venture into a world of technophilia, social inequality, corporate domination, and hacker culture. The game's key features include rich character customisation, thrilling gun battles, a deep narrative, and reflections on a futuristic society, attracting many gamers into the sci-fi genre.

Why cyberpunk2077 is an anticipant game for player?

Cyberpunk's appeal lies in its unique interpretation of the future, a world where technology is at its peak, but human life is full of conflicts and contradictions. Cyberpunk 2077 embodies this futuristic approach, inviting players to delve into a world that is as surreal as it is realistic.

The Cyberpunk 2077 game cost more than $174 million to develop, and marketing costs pushed the total investment to $314 million, making it one of the most expensive games in the world.

CD Projekt Red has been developing the game for eight years, from conception in 2012 to release in 2020. During that time, CD Projekt Red built its own game engine and designed a unique characterisation and narrative system. Cyberpunk 2077 was officially released in 2020 and had one of the highest number of pre-orders ever for a first release with 8 million copies on launch day.

Why is The Witcher series so anticipated as CD Projekt Red's next step? Mostly because of CD Projekt Red's narrative skills in characterisation and storytelling, whether it's Geralt of Rivia in The Witcher or V. After the release of Cyberpunk 2077, CD Projekt Red has announced a return to development of The Witcher series. The next instalment of The Witcher will be powered by the Unreal Engine 5 engine, which means higher quality visuals and more realistic immersion, further fuelling gamers' anticipation.
